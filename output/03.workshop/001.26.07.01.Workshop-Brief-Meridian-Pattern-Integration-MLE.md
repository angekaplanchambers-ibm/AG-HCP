# Workshop Brief: Meridian Pattern Integration into Terraform MLE

#### Ange Kaplan-Chambers

## Executive Summary

This workshop will align teams on a focused, near-term approach to integrating Meridian patterns and experiences into Terraform. The resulting discussion will drive decisions on which patterns to adopt, define an MLE (Minimum Loveable Experience) in the tightly constrained 6-month timeline, and establish a clear foundation for long-tail support.

## Workshop Brief: Meridian Integration into Terraform

This workshop focuses on how Meridian patterns can be applied directly within Terraform to guide near-term product development. Given limited time, stakeholder availability, and growing resource constraints, the session prioritizes practical decision-making over broad exploration.

Participants will evaluate specific Meridian patterns through concrete "what if" scenarios. The group will assess their value, feasibility and tradeoffs; then align on which patterns should be integrated and how they might take shape within Terraform. The discussion is intentionally design-led and grounded in real product considerations.

## Key Objective

Define a baseline experience that delivers strong user value with minimal complexity. The workshop will establish what cannot be compromised, ensuring that any future scope reductions remain intentional and aligned. From this foundation, the group will identify how the experience can evolve over time.

## Goal

By the end of the workshop, the team will align a prioritized set of patterns, a shared understanding of the MLE (Minimum Loveable Experience), and clear markers for progress. These outcomes will reduce ambiguity, promote cohesion, support Agentic integration to reduce churn risk, and strengthen confidence in Terraform through its relationship to Meridian.

## Shared Definitions

These definitions should be shared before the working session begins. Assuming shared understanding has caused confusion in prior planning sessions.

**Pattern** - An established micro-interaction or visual feature already built into a piece of software that can be lifted and applied elsewhere. Either experiential (a call-and-response loop, a decision trail, a multi-step workflow) or visual (an interactive panel, a chat surface, a progress summary). Not a feature - a repeatable interaction shape.

**MLE (Minimum Loveable Experience)** - The smallest set of patterns and interactions that must be present for the experience to deliver meaningful value. Not a feature list. The feeling the user gets - confidence, control, transparency - is the unit of measurement. Everything outside the MLE is a candidate for deferral.

**Meridian vs AGX** - Meridian is the interactive layer: the thing the user sees, the back-and-forth, the checks and balances, the decision trail. AGX is the backend orchestration platform: what coordinates and triggers agents behind the scenes. Users experience Meridian. Users do not see AGX. This workshop is about Meridian's interaction patterns only.

## Design Principles for Evaluation

Use these to evaluate each "what if" scenario. A pattern that conflicts with these principles should be rejected or deferred.

**Trophies on a mantle, not a laundry list.** The goal of TFE's agentic surface is to show users what has been done for them automatically - what problems the platform solved, what failures it caught, what drift it remediated. Not to give users more controls to operate. Every pattern evaluation should ask: does this add to the user's workload, or does it reduce it?

**Trust is earned through visibility.** Users will not trust agent actions they cannot see, explain, or audit. Every interaction pattern must make it clear what the agent read, what it proposed, and what was approved. Transparency is not optional - it is the mechanism by which trust is built incrementally.

**Micro-automations before macro-automations.** Users will never accept fully autonomous production changes in the first version. Probably not in the second. The first patterns we adopt should handle small, low-risk, high-confidence actions with clear human review gates. Larger automation scope is unlocked by demonstrated trust in smaller scope.

**Immersive over injected.** Individual AI features dropped onto pages ("inject a chat box here") produce fragmented experiences. The goal is a cohesive immersive experience - an agent workspace that feels like it belongs in TFE, not bolted on. Pattern evaluation should favor patterns that contribute to a coherent immersive whole.

## Proposed Workshop Schedule (90min)

The workshop will briefly align on purpose, scope, and constraints, reinforcing the focus on near-term Meridian integration into Terraform and the decisions required.
Then the group will review a targeted set of Meridian patterns in context to establish a shared baseline before evaluation begins.
Much of the session will be a structured, design-led discussion centered on "what if" scenarios. Participants will assess the value, tradeoffs, and feasibility of integrating specific patterns, driving alignment on what moves forward.
Then we shift to defining the MLE (Minimum Loveable Experience), identifying the essential elements that must be preserved to deliver meaningful value under current constraints.
Then concludes with a clear synthesis of prioritized patterns, baseline experience, and agreed markers of progress to guide execution over the next six months.

## Workshop Milestones (90min)

#### Introduction and Alignment (5min)

Align on purpose, scope, and constraints. Reinforce the focus on near-term Meridian integration into Terraform and clarify expected outcomes for the session.

#### Meridian Patterns Review (20min)

Review a curated set of Meridian patterns within the Terraform context to establish a shared baseline and ground the discussion in concrete examples.

#### Pattern Evaluation and "What If" Scenarios (35min)

Conduct a structured, design-led discussion evaluating how specific Meridian patterns could be layered into Terraform, focusing on value, feasibility, and tradeoffs. Drive toward alignment on which patterns should move forward.

#### Define Minimum Loveable Experience (15min)

Identify the core elements that cannot be compromised, establishing a baseline experience that delivers meaningful value within current resource constraints.

#### Synthesis and Next Steps (10min)

Confirm prioritized patterns, align on the baseline experience, and establish clear markers of progress to guide execution over the next six months.
