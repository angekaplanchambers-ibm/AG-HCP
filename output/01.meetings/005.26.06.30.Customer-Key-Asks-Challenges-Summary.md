# 005.26.06.30.Customer-Key-Asks-Challenges-Summary

## TL;DR

Across 14 named accounts, the dominant customer ask is not generic AI chat - it is AI-driven infrastructure workflows built around Terraform, module registries, policies, testing, CI/CD, and platform engineering processes. The five most common themes are: faster module creation, easier module consumption and self-service, natural-language infrastructure requests, migration acceleration from CloudFormation/Bicep/CDK, and governance/testing/compliance as a prerequisite for any AI adoption. Module intelligence is the most cross-cutting gap. Top five lighthouse candidates are CBA, Westpac, Salesforce DET, Oxy, and Bank of America.

This document materially changes the weight of the module-backed intent-to-IaC work in the strategy doc - it is not a supporting CUJ. It is the dominant customer ask. It also confirms the urgency behind Omar's level-one primitives argument: customers are ready to use level-two workflows, but the underlying primitives (module semantic summaries, private registry context, workspace data) are not yet in place.

## Key Discussion Points

### 1. Five cross-customer themes

The document identifies five repeating themes across the 14 accounts:

**Theme 1: Faster Terraform module creation**
Platform teams spend too long building, updating, testing, and releasing compliant modules. Customers want AI to reduce that cycle.
Accounts: Westpac, CBA, Clover, Pickles, PMI, Applied Materials

**Theme 2: Easier module consumption / self-service**
App teams cannot find and consume approved modules without deep Terraform expertise or heavy platform-team handholding. Customers want self-service discovery and consumption.
Accounts: KiwiBank, Westpac, CBA, Oxy, Elevance, Clover, Salesforce DET

**Theme 3: Natural-language infrastructure requests**
Users want to request infrastructure in Slack or chat, and have the system generate Terraform/config, tests, PRs, and compliance checks.
Accounts: Salesforce DET, Oxy, Bank of America, Westpac

**Theme 4: Migration acceleration**
AI-assisted conversion from CloudFormation, Bicep, CDK, or unmanaged workloads into Terraform/TFE/HCP Terraform.
Accounts: CBA, Booking.com, Pickles, Bank of America

**Theme 5: Governance, testing, compliance, and safety**
Customers want AI acceleration but will not adopt it without policy-as-code, approval workflows, security controls, identity, auditability, and testing in place.
Accounts: Westpac, Clover, Fifth Third Bank, Bank of America, Salesforce DET

### 2. The repeatable platform workflow customers are describing

The document names this explicitly:

```
request → discover approved module → generate Terraform/config → generate tests
→ open PR → validate policy/security → deploy through TFC/HCP Terraform → manage Day 2 changes
```

This is not a single feature. It is the full loop the strategy doc's product promise was designed around. Customer demand is confirming the loop, not individual pieces.

### 4. Biggest product and solution gaps named

- **Module intelligence:** AI needs deep context from private module registry, module docs, examples, versions, provider constraints, policy rules, and existing workspace patterns. Current MCP-style lookup by name is insufficient.
- **Testing automation:** Westpac specifically highlights Terraform test generation and validation as high-value and concrete. 350+ modules without automated test generation is the pain.
- **Enterprise control plane / MCP story:** Customers ask where MCP servers run, how remote hosting works, how identity works, and how this connects to TFE/HCP Terraform, Vault, Nomad, and InfraGraph.
- **Security and governance:** Banks and regulated customers want agentic workflows only if identity, access, approvals, auditability, and policy-as-code are clear and visible.
- **Services packaging:** Several accounts need RS/PS engagement. The opportunity likely requires a packaged "AI readiness + workflow implementation" engagement, not just a demo or repo handoff.

## Workshop implications

- **Module work must be elevated.** The workshop should not treat module-backed intent-to-IaC as a downstream CUJ. Customer evidence puts it at the top of the demand signal.
- **Lighthouse candidates give the workshop concrete anchors.** CBA, Westpac, Salesforce DET, Oxy, and Bank of America should be named in the workshop as the five accounts the first proof must be credible to.

- **Testing automation is more concrete than it appears in the strategy doc.** Westpac's 350+ module backlog and explicit request for Terraform test generation is a specific, buildable use case with a named customer.
