# 014.26.06.29.Meridian-Immersion-Paul-Ange

## TL;DR

This 1:1 between Paul (Meridian PM Director) and Ange is the foundational context-setting session for the Meridian/Terraform integration strategy. Five things from this conversation materially change prior pipeline assumptions:

1. **AGX will probably mostly go away.** Paul expects consolidation around Meridian. This directly challenges the AGX-as-definitive-backend assumption in strategy docs 003 and 004.
2. **Z/mainframe is 30-60% of HashiCorp revenue.** The Meridian-for-Z project is not a side bet - it is the primary revenue driver and the reason Meridian is getting built at all.
3. **TFC integration is Q1-Q2 2027.** The six-month window (by HashiConf or December) is effectively a TFE story, not TFC.
4. **The TFC/Meridian integration is primarily a UX problem.** Paul's words. This is what design owns.

Paul endorses Ange's workshop direction toward Meridian-first integration into TF and the "immersive experience" framing.

---

## Context

1:1 session. Paul is PM Director over two PMs working on Meridian. Ange had previously sent Paul the Meridian PRD and Business Case documents. This session was Paul walking Ange through the full Meridian context: Z delivery, go-to-market packaging, organizational history, AGX relationship, and TFC timeline. Date: 2026-06-29.

---

## Key Discussion Points

### 1. Meridian's current state: Z delivery is the only focus

The Meridian team is entirely focused on the Z (IBM mainframe) delivery right now. Timeline is tight. Paul does not want to distract the team, but believes they can burn through things quickly with their current roster. Non-Z options (TFE, TFC) will open up soon.

### 2. Go-to-market packaging

| Deployment | Model | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| Z (mainframe) | Ship Meridian as standalone; bundle with Terraform for Z initially; buy TF → get Meridian; later sold separately → installs TFE + Vault Enterprise | This year |
| Power (IBM Power PC) | Same posture as Z packaging | Same |
| TFE (existing customers) | Same packaging - Meridian as separate add-on for customers who want AI support | TBD |
| TFC (SaaS cloud) | Both products come together; the real integration; where design and UX work is needed | Q1-Q2 2027 |

The intent for TFE/TFC is: you buy Terraform and get Meridian. Eventually Meridian is a separate purchasable product that installs TFE + Vault for you.

### 3. Z revenue context

Z and the mainframe Terraform provider alone represent 30-60% of HashiCorp revenue. Companies are spending millions of dollars on just the Terraform provider for Z. The Z team had been waiting approximately three years for something like this; Meridian jumped ahead of where they would have gone. IBM CIO office is an early tester.

Physical install context: Z machines cost ~$500k each. Installation is physical, not cloud. IBM CIO office is first access point.

### 4. AGX consolidation signal

Paul: "I think the AGX stuff will probably mostly go away."

His reasoning: Meridian is what should power all AI features across Terraform and different cloud. The various AI teams should be reoriented toward Meridian rather than running separate initiatives. He acknowledges AGX team has done good work but expects consolidation.

**Pipeline flag**: This directly contradicts the foundation of strategy docs 003 and 004, which treat AGX as the definitive backend agent runtime. If AGX consolidates into Meridian, the layer separation doc (003) and the AGX readiness map (004) need to be revisited after the workshop.

### 5. Two tracks of platform work

| Track | What it means |
|---|---|
| Make Terraform better for agents | CLI improvements, API coverage, data accessibility, agent skills/knowledge packs |
| Make agents better at operating Terraform | Meridian cloud operations bootstrap, CSP coverage (AWS, GCP, Azure, OCI), simulation layer |

For TFC, Meridian needs to be bootstrapped to understand cloud operations - different from Z-specific knowledge. This work involves adding CSP support, testing, and potentially acquiring a team that has already wrapped AWS APIs for simulation.

### 6. TFC integration is a UX problem

Paul's framing: integrating Meridian and TFC together is primarily a UX problem. Not a backend problem. The technical pieces exist or are being built; the design work of how to surface it in TFC is the gap. This is where Ange and Gabrielle's work lives.

Two modes of integration:
- **Injected**: individual Meridian capabilities dropped into specific TFC UI pages
- **Immersive**: full Meridian experience exposed inside TFC somewhere - an immersive agent workspace

Paul wants the immersive experience, not just injection.
