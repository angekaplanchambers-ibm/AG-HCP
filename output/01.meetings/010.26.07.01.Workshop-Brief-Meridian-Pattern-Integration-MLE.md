## Key Discussion Points

### 1. Framing: constraint-first, not exploration-first

The brief explicitly names the operating conditions: limited time, stakeholder availability, growing resource constraints. The session is designed to make decisions under these constraints, not to expand scope. This framing is consistent with Omar's level-one-first argument (004) and the strategy doc's six-month scope boundary (002).

### 2. Method: "what if" scenario evaluation

The core 35-minute block is structured around "what if" scenarios per Meridian pattern. Participants assess value, feasibility, and tradeoffs. This is the design-led equivalent of the engineering feasibility questions in the interview scripts (003) - it surfaces the same dependency and constraint questions from a pattern-first angle rather than a platform-first angle.

### 3. MLE definition: what cannot be compromised

The 15-minute MLE block is the highest-stakes output of the session. It produces the non-negotiable baseline - the set of patterns and interactions that must be present for the experience to deliver meaningful value. Everything outside the MLE is a candidate for deferral. This directly feeds the PDR-004 wireframe plan: PDR-004 should only include MLE-validated components.

### 4. Intended outcomes

Three concrete outputs the brief names:
- Prioritized set of Meridian patterns for Terraform integration
- Shared understanding of the MLE (what cannot be cut)
- Clear markers of progress to guide execution over the next six months

### 5. Schedule structure (90 minutes)

| Segment | Time | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Introduction and Alignment | 5 min | Align on scope, constraints, expected outcomes |
| Meridian Patterns Review | 20 min | Shared baseline: curated patterns in Terraform context |
| Pattern Evaluation and "What If" Scenarios | 35 min | Assess value, feasibility, tradeoffs per pattern; drive alignment |
| Define Minimum Loveable Experience | 15 min | Name what cannot be compromised; set the baseline |
| Synthesis and Next Steps | 10 min | Confirm prioritized patterns, MLE, and progress markers |

## Implications for existing pipeline artifacts

### vs. Strategy doc (002)

The MLE definition this workshop produces should be compared against the strategy doc's "In Scope" list. Any MLE item not currently in scope should be added. Any in-scope item the workshop rules out of MLE should be moved to the scope backlog with a note.

### vs. Execution readiness map (004)

The "what if" scenarios should be grounded in the AGX priority gates from `004`. When evaluating a Meridian pattern, the feasibility question is not abstract - it is: "Is the AGX capability that this pattern requires available, in progress, or deferred?" The readiness map already answers this for all five AGX priorities.

### vs. Interview scripts (003)

The workshop is design-led; the interviews with Chris Audie, Omar Ismail, and Daniel Savage are PM/strategy-led. The MLE definition from this workshop should be shared with Omar and Daniel before or during their interviews to test whether the design-assessed MLE aligns with the PM-assessed level-one scope.

### vs. PDR chain (not yet started)

The MLE output from this workshop is the direct input to PDR-004 (wireframe plan). PDR-004 should only include components that cleared the MLE evaluation. Running the PDR chain before this workshop runs risks specifying wireframes for components that may be cut.
