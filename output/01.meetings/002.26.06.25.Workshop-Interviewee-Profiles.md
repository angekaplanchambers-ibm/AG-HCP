# 002.26.06.25-Workshop-Interviewee-Profiles

## TL;DR

Three HashiCorp product leaders have been identified as workshop interviewees. Daniel Savage (Senior Director, Terraform PM) owns the Terraform product strategy and execution. Chris Audie (SVP Product Management) owns P&L, overall product strategy, and GTM across the HashiCorp portfolio. Omar Ismail (Product Manager) operates at the feature execution level within the same org. All three report into the IBM Software / HCP org under Michael Weingartner. The interviews should set context on strategic priorities, decision authority, and ground-level feature concerns before the agentic direction is workshopped.

## Context

Profile document prepared ahead of a workshop to align the agentic HCP Terraform direction. The three individuals will be interviewed to help set context for discussion on next steps. All sit in the HashiCorp Product Management department under IBM Software.

## Key Discussion Points

### Daniel Savage - Senior Director, Terraform Product Management

**Role context:** Operates at the product manager level with Terraform-specific scope. Responsible for product strategy, roadmap, competitive analysis, pricing, ecosystem, and channel strategy for Terraform. Manages product lifecycle from inception through maturity. Interfaces with design, engineering, sales, product marketing, and ecosystem partners.

**Relevance to workshop:** Savage is the likely owner of the near-term Terraform roadmap decisions and the execution plan for any agentic feature work. He is the most direct stakeholder for the vertical slice prioritization (failed-run repair vs. module-backed intent vs. drift remediation) and for the Airbnb / competitive pressure framing from the documentation.

**Interview angle:** What does the current Terraform roadmap have committed for agentic work? Where is the execution dependency (Atlas, platform, MCP team)? What does "credible motion" look like from his perspective given the Airbnb signal?

---

### Chris Audie - SVP Product Management, HashiCorp

**Role context:** Executive-level scope covering P&L, strategy, GTM execution, product investments, and business development across the HashiCorp portfolio. Also carries a software development executive responsibility - driving strategy, development, and maintenance of software deliverables, including investment decisions on new and existing features.

**Relevance to workshop:** Audie sets the investment framing and business case. Any multi-quarter agentic build requires his alignment on product investment and GTM execution. He is the person who would approve or constrain the six-month scope. His framing of the competitive urgency (Spacelift/Airbnb) shapes how urgently the organization moves.

**Interview angle:** What is the business case framing for agentic HCP Terraform investment? How does the Airbnb churn signal land at the SVP level? What does success look like at 6 months from a business result perspective (revenue, retention, market signal)?

### Omar Ismail - Product Manager

**Role context:** Product Manager operating at the feature execution level within the same Hashicorp Product Management org. Responsible for bringing products to market through strategy definition and execution plans. Interfaces with design, engineering, sales, and ecosystem partners. Scope is product-level rather than portfolio-level.

**Relevance to workshop:** Ismail represents the ground-level PM perspective - the person closest to day-to-day feature decisions, user feedback, and execution constraints. His specific domain ownership within the org is not stated in the profile; this should be confirmed at the start of the interview. He is likely the most useful voice for understanding what is actually feasible in the near term and what users are telling the team directly.

**Interview angle:** What specific area of HCP Terraform does he own? What does he hear most frequently from users about pain points? Does the failed-run repair framing match what he observes? What execution concerns would he raise about the proposed first slice?

## Interview question recommendations

Drawn from `04-workshop-questions-and-validation.md`. Prioritized per interviewee role.

**For Daniel Savage (Terraform PM execution focus):**

1. What is the first flagship slice - is failed-run repair the right proof point?
2. Which HCP Terraform surfaces can change without Atlas/platform dependency risk?
3. Which run, plan, log, state, policy, and workspace data is accessible to the agent today?
4. Can the agent create branches, PRs, speculative runs, and registry/module test evidence today?
5. What must be visible in the first demo to move the team?

**For Chris Audie (SVP strategy and investment focus):**

1. What would HCP Terraform need to show in 6 months to feel credible again to accounts like Airbnb?
2. What does "agentic workflows" mean from a GTM perspective - is it a new product tier, a feature, or a platform capability?
3. Which competitor move changed internal expectations most: Spacelift Intent, Pulumi Neo, GitHub Copilot, or something else?
4. What is the business result metric for success: retention, new ARR, market positioning, or analyst recognition?
5. How should HCP Terraform show up inside existing assistants (Copilot, Claude, Cursor, Slack) vs. primarily inside the HCP Terraform UI?

**For Omar Ismail (PM execution and user feedback focus):**

1. What specific area of HCP Terraform do you own?
2. What user or customer feedback have you heard most frequently about pain points in the current Terraform workflow?
3. Does failed-run repair feel like the right first slice, or is there a more urgent gap you would prioritize?
4. What would make the first agentic feature feel genuinely useful versus a tech demo?
5. What execution or feasibility concerns would you raise about the proposed first slice?
