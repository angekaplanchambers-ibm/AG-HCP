# Workshop Interview Guides

- **Date:** 2026-06-25
- **Purpose:** Pre-workshop interviews to surface concerns, priorities, and constraints that shape the agentic HCP Terraform workshop trajectory
- **Format:** Individual 30-minute sessions, one-on-one
- **Interviewer note:** These are discovery conversations, not presentations. Do not walk through the strategy doc or pre-brief the direction. Let each person surface their own frame first.

---

## Guide 1: Daniel Savage
### Senior Director, Terraform Product Management

---

### Who this is and what you are trying to learn

Daniel owns the Terraform product roadmap and execution plan. He is the most direct stakeholder for near-term build decisions. He knows what is technically feasible, what is already in motion, and where the real platform dependencies sit. He has likely the most direct exposure to customer pain at the product level.

The goal of this interview is to understand:

- What is already committed or in-flight on the agentic side so the workshop does not re-debate settled ground
- Where the hard constraints are - Atlas dependencies, platform coupling, data availability
- Which failure classes or customer pain points he considers the most urgent, which may differ from the documentation framing
- What level of agent autonomy he considers acceptable at first launch
- What he would need to see in a first demo or prototype to feel confident presenting it upward

Do not lead with "we think failed-run repair should be first." Ask what he thinks is most urgent and why.

---

### Time map

| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| 0-2 min | Brief framing: why you are meeting, what you will do with the input, confirmation the session is informal and exploratory |
| 2-24 min | 6-7 questions - roughly 3-4 minutes per question |
| 24-28 min | Open floor: anything you did not ask that he wants to raise |
| 28-30 min | Close: confirm how findings will feed the workshop, thank him |

---

### Questions

**Q1 - Current state (open the floor)**

> Before we get into the workshop direction, what is already in motion on the agentic side of Terraform? What has already been decided, committed, or scoped that we should treat as settled going into the workshop?

*Why this question:* Establishes what is not up for debate, and surfaces any commitments the documentation does not reflect.

*Follow-up if needed:*
- Is there anything already in development or in roadmap planning that overlaps with what we are discussing?
- Are there things the team has already tried and stepped back from?

---

**Q2 - Customer pain signal**

> From your vantage point, where are customers and internal teams experiencing the most acute pain with Terraform today - the kind of pain that, if you fixed it, would change a retention or adoption conversation?

*Why this question:* Surfaces his actual priority order, which may differ from or confirm the failed-run repair framing. Avoids leading him toward the documented slices.

*Follow-up if needed:*
- Is the Airbnb feedback representative of a broader pattern, or is it an outlier?
- Are there failure classes or workflow gaps that come up repeatedly in customer conversations that we are not currently tracking?
- Which of those feels most solvable in a six-month window?

---

**Q3 - Platform and dependency constraints**

> When you think about building agentic capabilities on top of HCP Terraform, where are the hardest dependencies - the places where what we want to do requires something else to change first?

*Why this question:* Uncovers the real constraints that might make a chosen slice harder or easier than it looks on paper. Atlas coupling, platform team dependencies, and data availability are assumed constraints that need validation.

*Follow-up if needed:*
- Are there surfaces or pages in HCP Terraform where change is relatively low-risk and fast to ship versus surfaces that require a lot of coordination?
- What data do we already have access to - runs, logs, plans, policy results - and where are the gaps?
- Is the existing Terraform MCP Server in a state that could support product-grade features, or does it need significant work first?

---

**Q4 - Trust and autonomy boundary**

> When you think about what is acceptable for an agent to do on behalf of a user in a production Terraform environment - where is the line? What actions need a human in the loop, and what can the agent do without asking?

*Why this question:* The trust model is the hardest design problem in the space and will drive both the UX and the engineering architecture. His instinct on this shapes the workshop framing significantly.

*Follow-up if needed:*
- Is that line the same for all customers, or does it vary by tier or use case?
- What would a customer need to see before they trusted an agent-proposed change enough to merge the PR?
- What would cause enterprise customers to reject the feature entirely if we got the trust model wrong?

---

**Q5 - Prioritization instinct**

> If you had to put the following in order by urgency - failed-run repair, drift remediation, module-backed intent-to-IaC, policy failure repair, and workspace lifecycle automation - what order would you pick and why?

*Why this question:* Tests the prioritization matrix from the documentation against his ground-level judgment. If his order differs, that is important workshop input.

*Follow-up if needed:*
- Is there anything missing from that list that you would add?
- Are any of those things actually the same problem expressed differently?
- Which one, if we got it right, would have the biggest competitive impact against Spacelift specifically?

---

**Q6 - First demo bar**

> What would a first prototype or demo need to show for you to feel confident presenting it to leadership or to a customer like Airbnb?

*Why this question:* Defines the minimum bar for the first proof point and surfaces any internal political or business context that shapes what "credible" means at this organization.

*Follow-up if needed:*
- What would make the demo feel like a chatbot bolted onto the UI, and what would make it feel like something genuinely different?
- Is there a specific moment in the demo that has to land for it to work?
- Are there things that should explicitly not be in the demo because they would undermine trust?

---

**Q7 - Workshop ask (if time permits)**

> Going into the workshop, is there a question you think the team needs to answer that we have been avoiding, or a constraint the team needs to confront directly instead of working around?

*Why this question:* Invites him to surface the uncomfortable thing. Often the most useful input.

---

### Closing

Thank him and confirm:

- These inputs will be used to frame the workshop, not to pre-decide it
- He will see the workshop agenda before it runs
- If anything surfaces after the call that he wants to add, he should send it directly

## Guide 2: Chris Audie
### SVP Product Management, HashiCorp

---

### Who this is and what you are trying to learn

Chris owns P&L, product strategy, GTM execution, and product investment decisions across the HashiCorp portfolio. He sets the business case frame and approves or constrains the scope of any significant build. He is not likely to be close to the technical constraints but will have strong and directional views on competitive urgency, what the market expects, and what business result justifies the investment.

The goal of this interview is to understand:

- How the competitive pressure (Spacelift, Airbnb account movement) registers at his level and what response it demands
- What business outcome would signal that the agentic investment worked at 6 months
- Whether the proposed scope (starting with failed-run repair) feels right-sized or too narrow given the business urgency
- What his concerns are about agent risk, enterprise trust, or GTM readiness
- Whether there are other priorities competing for the same investment that the workshop needs to account for

Do not walk him through a feature list. Start with the business situation and let him set the frame.

---

### Time map

| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| 0-2 min | Brief framing: why you are meeting, what you will do with the input, confirmation the session is informal and exploratory |
| 2-24 min | 6-7 questions - roughly 3-4 minutes per question |
| 24-28 min | Open floor: anything you did not ask that he wants to raise |
| 28-30 min | Close: confirm how findings will feed the workshop, thank him |

---

### Questions

**Q1 - Business situation read (open the floor)**

> How are you reading the current competitive situation for HCP Terraform - especially given what we are seeing from Spacelift and from accounts like Airbnb? What does that mean for how urgently the organization needs to move?

*Why this question:* Establishes his competitive urgency frame before you introduce any specific direction. His read on urgency shapes how much latitude the team has to be deliberate vs. needing to move fast.

*Follow-up if needed:*
- Is Airbnb a signal about a broader pattern, or is it account-specific?
- Are there other competitive moves you are watching that we should factor into the workshop?
- What does "credible motion" look like from where you sit - what would change your read on our position in the market?

---

**Q2 - Definition of success**

> If we make the right investments over the next six months, what does success look like - how would you know we got it right? What is the business result that matters most?

*Why this question:* Surfaces whether the success metric is retention, new ARR, analyst/market positioning, competitive differentiation, or something else entirely. The answer shapes how the workshop prioritizes and scopes.

*Follow-up if needed:*
- Is there a specific retention or churn signal that would confirm we are moving in the right direction?
- Is there an external signal - analyst report, conference narrative, competitor announcement - that would tell us whether we have established a credible position?
- What would failure look like at 6 months?

---

**Q3 - Scope of ambition**

> When you think about where HCP Terraform should be in this agentic wave, are you thinking about this as a feature we add, a capability that differentiates the platform, or something that fundamentally changes what HCP Terraform is?

*Why this question:* Surfaces the scope of ambition at the executive level. If he is thinking "fundamental platform change," a first slice focused on failed-run repair may feel too narrow. If he is thinking "credible differentiating capability," it may feel right. This misalignment, if it exists, needs to surface before the workshop.

*Follow-up if needed:*
- Does the answer change depending on which market segment we are talking about - enterprise accounts, mid-market, or developer-led?
- Are you thinking about this as something that ships inside HCP Terraform, or as something that also extends into the tools developers already use - Copilot, Claude, Cursor, Slack?
- Is there a version of this that could be a new product tier or offering, or should it be part of the existing platform?

---

**Q4 - Investment and competing priorities**

> What else is competing for the same engineering and product investment right now? What would we be trading off if we committed significant capacity to the agentic direction for the next six months?

*Why this question:* Surfaces the opportunity cost conversation that will constrain the workshop's scope decisions. If there are other bets he considers higher priority, the workshop needs to know that before it commits to a build plan.

*Follow-up if needed:*
- Are there commitments already made to customers or partners that shape what engineering capacity can be directed here?
- Is there an internal appetite to staff up or partner to move faster, or does this need to work within current headcount?
- Are there regulatory, compliance, or enterprise security concerns that would slow the agentic work down even if we had the capacity?

---

**Q5 - Enterprise trust and risk**

> What concerns you most about putting agentic capabilities into the hands of enterprise Terraform users? Where is the risk that could cause the most damage if we got it wrong?

*Why this question:* Enterprise trust is the hardest dimension to recover from if it breaks. His view on the risk frame shapes what the trust model, approval gates, and audit capabilities need to look like - and how conservative the first release needs to be.

*Follow-up if needed:*
- How much of that risk is about the agent making a wrong technical suggestion versus taking an unauthorized action?
- Is there a version of the trust model that makes the first release feel safe to the enterprise buyer without making it so conservative it is not useful?
- What would a customer incident related to agent-suggested infrastructure changes mean for the business?

---

**Q6 - GTM framing**

> How do you want to talk about this in the market - what is the one-line story that positions HCP Terraform's agentic direction against Spacelift, Pulumi, and the GitHub Copilot story?

*Why this question:* The GTM framing reveals what he thinks the differentiation actually is, which shapes the design and build direction more than any feature list. If he cannot say it in one line, that is useful signal too.

*Follow-up if needed:*
- Is "Terraform-native verified infrastructure intent" a frame that resonates, or does it need to be simpler or more benefit-oriented?
- Who is the audience for this story - the platform engineer, the CISO, the VP of Engineering, or the Terraform admin?
- Is there a competitor narrative we need to actively counter, or are we trying to define a new category?

---

**Q7 - Workshop ask (if time permits)**

> Going into the workshop, is there a business or strategic question you need the team to answer that has not been clearly put on the table yet?

*Why this question:* Invites him to surface the organizational or business constraint the team may be working around. His answer will likely be the most important input from the session.

---

### Closing

Thank him and confirm:

- These inputs will be used to frame the workshop - specifically to set the right scope and priority questions on the table
- He does not need to have reviewed any design or strategy materials ahead of the workshop
- If he has additional context after the call - customer conversations, board-level input, competitive intelligence - he should send it directly before the workshop runs
